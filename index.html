Police Shootings in the United States Since 2015

Demographics of police shootings since 2015. (As of 15th June 2020)

Which states have the most police shootings per capita?

The number of fatal police shootings in each U.S state have been divided by the population of the state to create this map. We see shootings are more dense in the west half of the U.S than the East. Contrary to common belief, New York has fewer shootings per capita than most states. New Mexico, Oklahoma, and Alaska have some of the most.

Who do police shoot, and where?

This map shows a dot for every recorded individual who has been fatally shot by a police officer since 2015. Hovering over will show their name and basic information such as race and age, as well as when they were killed. Some famous names that made major news stories have been annotated on the map. Colour of each dot indicates race, and these can be filtered by clicking on the legend.

What races are most affected by police shootings?

White people make up the majority of police shootings, followed by black and then hispanic/latinx people.
However if you account for what portion of the total population each race represents, then black, native, and then hispanic/latinx people are fatally shot by police far more than white people. (Click the legend to focus on one race)
